CMS Onboarding Redesign

I led a comprehensive redesign of the onboarding experience for new Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services employees entering a high-stakes, federal workplace.
Through user research, journey mapping, and iterative design, I transformed a fragmented, confusing process into a centralized, accessible system that reduced confusion and improved confidence for new hires.

Confidentiality Notice: Due to federal privacy restrictions, the redesigned modules are confidential and cannot be shown publicly.
However, I can present the high-level process, design rationale, and outcomes of this work.

Project Goal

The core objective was to improve clarity, accessibility, and workflow efficiency while reducing confusion for new employees onboarding into a highly technical federal environment. Effective onboarding for these employees is critical; new CMS employees directly impact systems that millions of Americans rely on, including HealthCare.gov.

The redesign focused on creating a streamlined, intuitive experience that would help new hires navigate complex systems, understand their new roles, and access critical resources without feeling overwhelmed.

My Role

As the lead researcher and designer, I owned the end-to-end human-centered design process:

I facilitated three focus groups with new hires and managers, conducted a comprehensive audit of existing onboarding materials, and mapped the complete onboarding journey to identify pain points.
I then designed and built the new system in Articulate Rise, creating a modular, interactive onboarding hub.
I also implemented a plan for a sustainable feedback and maintenance workflow, including a plan to make ongoing quarterly updates to the modules.

Understanding Needs

Through the three focus groups I conducted, I uncovered significant challenges that were hindering new hires' ability to contribute effectively:

  • Information was scattered across multiple platforms with no central hub to access all information
  • Confusing acronyms and unclear roles created barriers to understanding
  • Security access + clearance delays prevented timely access to critical resources [for over 3+ weeks]
  • Outdated documents and broken links led to frustration and misinformation
  • Unclear information about IT/security contacts created bottlenecks when issues arose
  • Inconsistent buddy system meant support varied dramatically between teams
  • Managers themselves were unsure of the onboarding workflow, creating confusion and inconsistency in upper-level support

New hires felt overwhelmed by the lack of accessible, digestible information and were frustrated by the lack of clear pathways.
Key themes identified: information overload, inaccessible systems, inconsistent support

Redesign Process

Define

I identified root causes: no central hub, outdated content, unclear workflow, and inconsistent implementation across teams.

Design

I built a modular, interactive onboarding hub in Articulate Rise. Content was clustered into structured modules, language was simplified, visuals were added for clarity. All resources were centralized in one accessible location.

Deliver

I tested the new system with new hires, gathered feedback, iterated on the structure, and built a feedback loop to ensure the system could be updated and maintained over time.

Key Improvements

  • Centralized onboarding hub eliminated scattered information
  • New platform allowed for more interactive and engaging content
  • Step-by-step path providing clear guidance available from Day 1 rather than Week 3 or beyond
  • Implemented a plan for scheduled document updates to ensure accuracy and relevance of information
  • Digital suggestion box enabling continuous improvement during quarterly updates

Impact

I conducted a post-implementation survey with new hires and managers to assess the impact of the redesign.
I found that the redesigned onboarding system delivered meaningful improvements across multiple dimensions:

  • New hires felt less overwhelmed and more confident entering their roles
  • Managers gained a consistent onboarding framework they could rely on
  • Sustainable, maintainable system established for long-term success
  • Increased clarity for employees, directly supporting better outcomes for millions relying on HealthCare.gov

What I Learned

This project marked my first time leading a full end-to-end human-centered design cycle. I was reminded of the power of simplifying complex systems-- impactful work isn't always flashy. Often, it's about making information humane and accessible.

I also discovered the critical importance of creating sustainable systems, not just one-time fixes. Building in feedback loops and maintenance workflows ensures the solution continues to serve users long after the initial launch.
